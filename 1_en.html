Speaking

Hello

"Hello" - Translation: "Salom"
Note: This is the most common greeting word. It can be used with someone you just met or in any situation.

Hey

"Hey" - Translation: "Hey" (Informal greeting)
Note: This is a more informal way of greeting. It is typically used between friends or acquaintances.

How are you?

"How are you?" - Translation: "Yaxshimisiz?"
Note: This question is used after greeting someone and is used to ask about the person's well-being.

How about you?

"How about you?" - Translation: "Sizniy qanday?"
Note: If someone asks about your well-being, you can use this question as a response to ask about theirs.

I’m great, thanks.

"I’m great, thanks." - Translation: "Men zo'rman, rahmat."
Note: This response is used for the "How are you?" question and indicates that the person feels very good.

I’m good, thanks.

"I’m good, thanks." - Translation: "Men yaxshi, rahmat."
Note: This is another response to the "How are you?" question, meaning "I’m good."

What’s your name?

"What’s your name?" - Translation: "Ismingiz nima?"
Note: This question is used to ask someone’s name when you meet them for the first time.

My name’s [name].

"My name’s [name]." - Translation: "Mening ismim [ism]."
Note: This is used to introduce yourself by stating your name.

Where are you from?

"Where are you from?" - Translation: "Qayerdansiz?"
Note: This question is used to ask where someone is from or their place of origin.

I’m from [city].

"I’m from [city]." - Translation: "Men [shahar]danman."
Note: This is used to express where you are from.

How about you?

"How about you?" - Translation: "Sizniy qanday?"
Note: After talking about your location, you ask about someone else’s.

Nice to meet you.

"Nice to meet you." - Translation: "Tanishganimdan xursandman."
Note: This phrase is used to politely greet someone when you first meet them.

You too.

"You too." - Translation: "Sizni ham."
Note: This is used as a polite response when you hear "Nice to meet you."
